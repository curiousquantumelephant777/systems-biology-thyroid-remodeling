## 🧪 Insight: Neurodivergence and EMK Upregulation Skewing Molecular Test

### Observations
This subject received a suspicious result on a Veracyte molecular test for thyroid nodules. One showed 11-year photographic stability, and both nodules were initially benign with reassuring topological characteristics (bland-appearing follicular cells arranged in sheets and clusters with healthy surrounding colloid fluid). The atypical snapshot of the second biopsy is hypothesized to have been caused by the perfect storm of the concurrent bacterial infection and phototoxic tissue injury.

### Genomic Hypothesis

The upregulation of the MAPK/ERK pathway is a downstream consequence of acute cellular stress, secondary to nearby bacterial infection and phototoxic tissue injury (with a baseline neurodivergent upregulation). This elevated ERK expression functions as transient biological noise capable of confounding baseline genomic sequencing and precipitating false-positive molecular test profiles. Red light therapy is also known to upregulate transcription, and the device I was using was faulty, which I think contributed. I'm also reading that MAPK/ERK signaling pathways are known to be upregulated in autism (I also feel significantly better and less inflamed after using berberine, which downregulates these pathways). I am interested in seeing if the rate of false positives with this test and elevated ERK/FMT expression might be relatively high in neurodivergent phenotypes. Also, I'm interested in analyzing how epigenomic changes (e.g., chronic enviornmental toxin/ethanol exposure, HPA-axis disruption due to stress, and chronic metabolic inflammation/pro-inflammatory macrophage shifts due to obesity) might also upregulate this genomic expression despite an indolent/benign nodular process.

### Discussion
Current commercial molecular diagnostic assays utilize machine-learning classifiers trained predominantly on neurotypical reference populations, introducing a systemic bias when analyzing neurodivergent phenotypes. Because conditions such as autism spectrum disorders are characterized by a constitutional, baseline upregulation of the MAPK/ERK signaling cascade and an elevated pro-inflammatory cytokine profile, these native homeostatic variations lower the transcriptomic threshold for hyper-activation. Consequently, when subjected to external stressors or low-risk somatic variants, these primed pathways generate highly amplified transcriptomic echoes. E

Standard algorithmic cutoffs misinterpret this phenotypic noise as active oncogenic proliferation, severely compromising the specificity of the test and driving the clinical over-management of entirely indolent lesions. This may lead to unnecessary surgery and denial of tissue-sparing procedures (e.g., radiofrequency ablation). These assays should be trained on more data from neurodivergent individuals, or repeat FNA/core needle biopsy may be indicated for neurodivergent phenotypes rather than molecular assays. Expanding the training data to include neurodivergent phenotypes would allow the machine-learning models to adjust their diagnostic cutoffs, drastically reducing false-positive "high risk" flags caused by native biological noise. 

Also, a research study should be conducted analyzing relative EMK expression in neurodivergent phenotypes with indeterminate nodules (Bethesda III/IV) that undergo Veracyte testing compared to neurotypicals to see if the baseline expression is higher, which should later be correlated with histological diagnosis post-resection and active surveillance/RFA outcomes.
